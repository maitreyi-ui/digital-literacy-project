## Project Overview
This project has been created as part of the Digital Literacy course. As a Student Digital Ambassador, the aim of this project is to understand and demonstrate the responsible use of digital tools, build a professional online presence, improve communication skills, and develop awareness about cyber safety.

The project consists of five tasks, each focusing on a different aspect of digital literacy.

---

## Task Summary

### Task 1 – Digital Literacy Infographic
I created a digital literacy infographic using Canva to explain important concepts in a simple and visually clear way. The infographic includes key topics such as the meaning of digital literacy, useful digital tools, safe internet practices, professional online presence, and email etiquette.

While designing the infographic, I focused on keeping the information short and easy to understand. Instead of writing long paragraphs, I used bullet points so that students can quickly read and understand the content. Canva helped me organise the layout properly and choose suitable colours and icons.

One challenge I faced was managing space because I had to include multiple topics in a single page without making it look crowded. This task helped me understand that digital literacy is not just about using tools, but also about using them responsibly and safely.
### Task 2 – Digital Portfolio
I created and updated my digital portfolio across three platforms: GitHub, LinkedIn, and Kaggle. Each platform serves a different purpose in building a professional online presence.

GitHub is mainly used for showcasing coding projects and tracking progress over time. I created a profile README to introduce myself and mention my academic background and interests. LinkedIn is a professional networking platform where I added my education details, which will help me connect with recruiters and professionals in the future. Kaggle is a platform for data science and practice-based learning, which I plan to explore further as I develop my technical skills.

Over the next four years, I plan to regularly update these platforms by adding projects, learning new skills, and building a strong professional profile. This task helped me understand the importance of maintaining an authentic and consistent digital presence.
### Task 3 – Coding & Collaboration Platforms
For this task, I explored both coding practice and collaboration tools. I created an account on HackerRank and completed a beginner-level problem to understand how coding platforms work. This helped me get familiar with problem-solving and basic programming logic.

I also created a Google Form titled “Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz” with five questions related to safe internet practices and digital knowledge. The form includes both multiple-choice and short-answer questions. Link o google form: https://forms.gle/8GyWug1ZZf9izmaU8

These tools will be helpful academically as coding platforms improve problem-solving skills, while Google Forms can be used for surveys, quizzes, and data collection in projects.
### Task 4 – Email Etiquette
One situation where poor digital communication can cause problems is when a student sends an unprofessional email to a professor or recruiter. For example, writing a message without a proper subject line, greeting, or polite tone can create a negative impression. The message may seem rude or careless, even if the intention was not wrong.

This issue can be avoided by following basic email etiquette, such as using a clear subject, addressing the recipient respectfully, and writing in a polite and structured manner. Proper communication reflects professionalism and increases the chances of receiving a positive response.

### Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness
While working on this task, I was surprised to see how easily phishing attacks can trick users by creating a sense of urgency and trust. Even simple-looking messages can appear very convincing if they include official logos and professional language.

This made me realise that awareness alone is not enough, and careful behaviour is equally important. One habit I plan to change is taking time to verify links and messages before responding, instead of reacting quickly. This will help me avoid potential cyber risks in the future.
