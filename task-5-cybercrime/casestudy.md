Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers try to trick users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or personal data by pretending to be a trusted source.

A common phishing scenario involves sending fake emails that appear to be from well-known companies or institutions. These emails often include official logos and urgent messages, such as claiming that the user has been selected for an internship or needs to verify their account immediately. The message usually contains a link that directs the user to a fake website that looks legitimate.

Students are often targeted because they actively look for opportunities such as internships, scholarships, and job offers. Due to excitement or urgency, they may click on the link without verifying its authenticity and enter their personal information.

The consequences of phishing can be serious. Attackers may gain access to email accounts, misuse personal data, or even carry out financial fraud. Victims may face loss of privacy, financial loss, and stress.

This shows that phishing attacks rely more on human behaviour than technical weaknesses, making awareness and careful decision-making essential.
